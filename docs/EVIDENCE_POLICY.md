# Evidence policy

## Source hierarchy

1. official standards, governments and professional bodies;
2. peer-reviewed literature;
3. full patent documents;
4. official software documentation and repositories;
5. company technical data;
6. textbooks and authoritative reports;
7. other sources as leads only.

## Required metadata

Each evidence record carries source type, title, locator, publication/effective date, retrieval date, grade, applicability, decision eligibility, contradiction status, review expiry and permitted design use.

## Decision use

Evidence may support screening, experiment design or design with review only when its boundary matches the active chemistry, materials, measurement method, scale and operating regime. Internet values and historical case parameters never become production settings automatically.

## Freshness and contradiction

Expired, superseded, retracted or unresolved contradictory evidence blocks dependent decisions. A newer source does not automatically dominate; evidence strength, experimental comparability and authority must be evaluated.
