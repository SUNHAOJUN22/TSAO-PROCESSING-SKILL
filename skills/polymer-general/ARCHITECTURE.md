# General polymer technology-development subskill

This subskill supplies route-neutral polymer R&D and industrialization logic. It routes to EPDM or POE when their domain signatures are present, but also supports radical, coordination, ionic, controlled/living, ring-opening, condensation, polyaddition, emulsion, suspension, bulk, solution, slurry, gas-phase and reactive-extrusion systems.

## Universal polymer causal chain

```text
application need
→ product CQA and molecular-architecture hypothesis
→ monomer/catalyst/initiator/material attributes
→ reaction network and transport
→ molecular-weight/composition/topology distribution
→ morphology and phase structure
→ rheology, conversion and finishing
→ formulation and processing
→ part performance
→ pilot and industrial capability
```

The project must preserve physical conservation and statistical consistency across this chain. Correlation can support screening, but a critical scale-up or product claim needs mechanism, designed perturbation, independent validation or explicitly bounded empirical evidence.

## Route-selection dimensions

- mechanism and active-species lifetime;
- heat-release rate and removable heat;
- phase state and thermodynamic stability;
- viscosity and mixing regime;
- solids/particle population and fouling tendency;
- molecular-weight, composition and topology control;
- catalyst/initiator residues and purification burden;
- solvent, water and monomer recovery;
- product form, devolatilization, drying and pelletization;
- batch/continuous controllability and transition waste;
- hazard, environmental, supply-chain and economic constraints.

## Scale hierarchy

The subskill distinguishes discovery microreactors, laboratory batch/semibatch work, continuous bench equipment, integrated pilot, demonstration and industrial operation. Advancement requires representative governing phenomena and a written scale-down model; scale alone never proves representativeness.

## Required project artifacts

Every project receives an evidence ledger, material passport, requirement–CQA–CPP trace, reaction-network register, analytical-method qualification, experiment master plan, model registry, uncertainty and applicability-domain record, scale-up claim ledger, hazard handoff, technical-economic/environmental basis, customer qualification plan, Gate record and change-impact graph.

## External execution boundary

The subskill prepares executable protocols and acceptance criteria but does not claim that laboratory experiments, commercial simulations, equipment engineering, safety studies, legal FTO, regulatory submissions, customer trials or industrial performance tests have occurred. Those statuses remain `NOT_EVALUATED` until evidence and approvals are attached.
