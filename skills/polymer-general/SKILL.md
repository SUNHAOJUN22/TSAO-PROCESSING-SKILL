# Universal Polymer Technology Development Subskill

Use this subskill for polymer projects that are not fully covered by EPDM or POE. It is mechanism- and process-route neutral.

## Supported chemistry families

- free-radical, controlled-radical and ionic polymerization;
- coordination and single-site olefin polymerization;
- polycondensation and polyaddition;
- ring-opening polymerization;
- interfacial, emulsion, suspension, slurry, bulk, solution and gas-phase systems;
- reactive extrusion, grafting, crosslinking, blending and formulation.

## Required causal chain

`product requirement → molecular architecture → reaction network → transport and phase behaviour → reactor → separation/finishing → morphology → rheology/processing → article performance → customer qualification`

For each route, define monomer and impurity specifications, catalyst or initiator chemistry, reaction heat, rate law, molecular-weight control, branching/crosslinking, phase behaviour, viscosity, mixing, heat and mass transfer, residuals, recovery, emissions and safe shutdown.

## Route-selection rule

Never choose a route from familiarity alone. Compare chemistry feasibility, product architecture controllability, intrinsic safety, raw-material and utility burden, separation difficulty, operability, scale history, supply chain, IP and lifecycle impact. Preserve at least one technically distinct counterfactual route until evidence closes the decision.

## Scale-up rule

A scale-up claim must state which dimensionless groups, residence-time characteristics, mixing times, heat-removal margins, phase states and molecular-architecture distributions are preserved; what similarity is broken; and what pilot evidence compensates for the break.

## Qualification boundary

A polymer specification, simulation or laboratory coupon is not customer qualification. Final approval requires representative conversion, additive package, processing history, article geometry, ageing protocol and use-condition evidence.
